Synths

  • Behringer Crave -- most songs on all of my albums from 2022 were recorded on this. I use it mostly for bass, as master clock and for experimenting.
  • Monotron duo -- my first synth, I haven't used it for a long time since I borrowed it, but now I have it back and I modded it so I use it as backing voice.
  • Monotron delay -- probably cheapest delay effect on the market with free synth included. I use the delay on most tracks. I also modded it so I used it as backing voice. Its also great for travelling since its small.
  • PO-33 -- my wild card, I use it mostly for drums since I don't have analog drum machine. I also use its massive sampling capabillities (e.g on my album weak).
  • Volca Keys -- hands-on instrument. I can make any sound in seconds. I use it mostly for chords, bass and lead
  • Passive mixer -- I've build simple passive 5 channel mixer with attenuators. It does the job, but it sometimes breaks.
  • Modular -- I'm working on custom DIY modular, becuase its best way to build custom synts.
  • Keystep -- for controlling crave and volca, I'm running midi from it thru crave to volca, where each synth has different channel.
  • Kastle ARP -- my exploration synth, DIYed at bastl party
  • SoftPop SPII -- beast synth that is very performative
